Living Ground. The Verdant Lady can move through nonmagical difficult terrain made of plants without expending extra movement, and such terrain doesn't hinder her allies. Each round at the start of her turn, she can designate one 10-foot cube of ground within 60 feet of her as fertile; it remains overgrown difficult terrain for the rest of the encounter.
Bloom of Renewal. Once per round when an ally of the Verdant Lady ends its turn standing on ground she has made fertile (see Living Ground), that ally regains 10 hit points.
Patient Gardener. The Verdant Lady has advantage on saving throws against any effect that would force her to act hastily or against her will, such as compulsion or haste-based magic — she has never once been rushed into anything.
Innate Spellcasting. The Verdant Lady's spellcasting ability is Wisdom (spell save DC 18). She can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: At will: entangle, goodberry, speak with plants 3/day each: plant growth, wall of thorns 1/day each: awaken
Legendary Resistance (2/Day). If the Verdant Lady fails a saving throw, she can choose to succeed instead.
Actions
Multiattack. The Verdant Lady makes two Thornlash attacks.
Thornlash. Melee Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, reach 15 ft. (vine-extended), one target. Hit: 18 (3d8 + 5) piercing damage, and the target is grappled (escape DC 18) if it is Large or smaller. While grappled this way, the target is restrained by living vines.
Call the Garden (Recharge 5–6). The Verdant Lady causes a 20-foot-radius circle of ground within 60 feet of her to erupt with grasping roots and blooming thorns. Each creature in that area must make a DC 18 Dexterity saving throw, taking 27 (6d8) piercing damage on a failure, or half as much on a success. The area becomes fertile ground (see Living Ground) regardless of the save.
Wall of Blossoms. The Verdant Lady causes a wall of dense, flowering bramble to grow in a location she chooses within 60 feet, up to 30 feet long, 10 feet high, and 5 feet thick. The wall is difficult terrain and blocks line of sight. It has AC 5, 60 hit points, vulnerability to fire damage, and resistance to bludgeoning and piercing damage. She can have only one Wall of Blossoms active at a time; creating a new one ends the old one.
The Greenbower Bleeds Through (Recharge 6). The Verdant Lady reaches for the boundary between Mirrorbloom and her realm and tears it thin. Vines split tile and stone from beneath within a 30-foot radius centered on her, blossoms erupting through the cracks within seconds as the architecture itself disappears under fast-climbing growth. This transformation lasts until the end of the encounter, and the entire area counts as fertile ground (see Living Ground). A Bower Thorn unfurls from a fresh hedge and acts on the Verdant Lady's initiative count. Allies who end their turn in the area regain 10 hit points (as Bloom of Renewal, in addition to any other instance of that trait); however, the growth is difficult terrain for every creature present, the Verdant Lady included, and it cannot be cleared by mundane means for the rest of the encounter.
Legendary Actions

The Verdant Lady can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. She regains spent legendary actions at the start of her turn.

Vine Step. The Verdant Lady moves up to half her speed without provoking opportunity attacks. This movement can include climbing on plants or walls of her own creation.
Lash. The Verdant Lady makes one Thornlash attack.
Sudden Bloom (Costs 2 Actions). The Verdant Lady causes flowers to burst from the ground beneath one creature within 60 feet. If that creature is an ally, it regains 15 hit points. If it is an enemy, it must succeed on a DC 18 Constitution saving throw or be restrained until the end of its next turn by sudden, fast-growing stems.
Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the Verdant Lady can take a lair action to cause one of the following effects; she can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

The Garden Provides. Goodberries sprout within reach of each of the Verdant Lady's allies in the Greenbower; any ally who eats one regains 4 hit points.
Reaching Roots. Roots burst from the ground in a 20-foot radius centered on a point the Verdant Lady chooses within her lair. Each creature hostile to her in that area must succeed on a DC 16 Strength saving throw or be restrained until the end of its next turn.
Generous Canopy. Leaves and branches knit together overhead, granting the Verdant Lady and her allies half cover against ranged attacks until initiative count 20 on the next round.
