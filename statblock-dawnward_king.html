First Light, Unremembered. At the start of each of the Dawnward King's turns, roll a d6 to determine a damage type he resists until the start of his next turn: 1 fire, 2 cold, 3 lightning, 4 thunder, 5 necrotic, 6 radiant. He has no memory of which type he resisted last round and the roll is always fresh.
No Yesterday. The Dawnward King cannot be frightened, and he is immune to any effect that would target him based on actions he took on a previous turn (such as a spell that requires identifying a repeated attack). At the start of each of his turns, any condition currently affecting him that allows a save at the end of each turn instead ends automatically, as he has already forgotten why it was applied — except conditions imposed during the current round.
Every Day, Anew. At the start of combat and at the start of each of his turns thereafter, the Dawnward King's player (or the DM) secretly rolls 1d4 to determine his attack pattern for the round from the Actions list below, numbered 1 to 4. He always uses this freshly rolled option rather than a chosen tactic, representing his total inability to form a strategy from one moment to the next.
Borrowed Morning. Once per turn when the Dawnward King would take damage, he can choose to treat that damage as though it happened 'yesterday' — reducing it by half, as the wound hasn't fully caught up to a body that doesn't remember being struck.
Innate Spellcasting. The Dawnward King's spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 19). He can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: At will: dancing lights, light, faerie fire 3/day each: daylight, haste 1/day each: sunburst
Legendary Resistance (3/Day). If the Dawnward King fails a saving throw, he can choose to succeed instead.
Actions

On his turn, roll 1d4 (or the DM secretly chooses) to determine which of the following the Dawnward King uses; see Every Day, Anew.

1. Radiant Greatblade (Multiattack). The Dawnward King makes two attacks with a blade of solid morning light. Melee Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 19 (3d8 + 6) radiant damage.
2. Sudden Sunrise. The Dawnward King unleashes a burst of new light. Each creature within 20 feet must make a DC 17 Constitution saving throw, taking 22 (5d8) radiant damage on a failure, or half as much on a success. Creatures that fail are also blinded until the end of their next turn.
3. Wandering Step. The Dawnward King, genuinely unsure why he came this way, moves up to his speed in a straight line chosen by the DM toward the nearest creature he hasn't yet noticed, or randomly if all creatures are visible to him. He makes one Radiant Greatblade attack against the first creature he reaches.
4. First Question. The Dawnward King asks a target within 30 feet a genuine, innocent question about itself ("Who are you? Have we met?"). The target must succeed on a DC 17 Wisdom saving throw or be charmed for 1 minute, unable to bring itself to attack someone so guilelessly curious. A creature can repeat the save if it takes damage.
The First Morning Bleeds Through (Recharge 6). The Dawnward King reaches for the boundary between Mirrorbloom and his realm and tears it thin. The ground underfoot loses its history — old stains, scorch marks, and wear vanish as the floor itself seems to wake up fresh, hills of pale new grass pushing up through stone, wood, or tile within a 30-foot radius centered on him. This transformation lasts until the end of the encounter. A First-Morning Sprite emerges from the new growth and acts on the Dawnward King's initiative count. While the transformed area persists, every creature within it has advantage on saving throws against being frightened, as nothing here remembers fear yet — but the terrain itself is unstable: at the start of each creature's turn in the area, the DM may move that creature 10 feet in a random direction (no action required, no opportunity attack provoked).
Legendary Actions

The Dawnward King can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. He regains spent legendary actions at the start of his turn.

Bright Step. The Dawnward King moves up to half his speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
Flash of Now. The Dawnward King makes one Radiant Greatblade attack.
Reroll the Day (Costs 2 Actions). The Dawnward King immediately re-rolls his First Light, Unremembered damage resistance and gains advantage on his next saving throw.
