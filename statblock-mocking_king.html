Setting Up the Bit. Whenever the Mocking King hits a creature with Cruel Claws, that creature gains 1 stack of Setup (maximum 3), representing how thoroughly he has read their fear. Stacks last until the end of combat or until spent (see Punchline).
Punchline. When the Mocking King hits a creature that has at least 1 stack of Setup, he can spend any number of stacks on that creature as part of the same attack. Each stack spent adds 1d10 extra psychic damage and reduces the target's speed by 10 feet until the end of its next turn, frozen by the cruelty of timing it never saw coming.
The Smile Doesn't Move. The Mocking King's expression never changes regardless of damage taken, conditions imposed, or circumstance, and he is immune to any effect that relies on reading or being read through facial expression, such as certain divination effects keying off emotional state. Creatures attempting to use Insight against him have disadvantage.
Stitched Patience. The Mocking King has advantage on saving throws against being incapacitated, paralyzed, or stunned — whatever was done to fix his smile in place also fixed something in him that no longer flinches.
Legendary Resistance (3/Day). If the Mocking King fails a saving throw, he can choose to succeed instead.
Actions
Multiattack. The Mocking King makes three Cruel Claws attacks.
Cruel Claws. Melee Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, reach 10 ft. (unnaturally long limbs), one target. Hit: 16 (2d8 + 7) slashing damage. See Setting Up the Bit and Punchline.
Forced Performance (Recharge 5–6). The Mocking King forces up to three creatures he can see within 30 feet to make a DC 18 Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, a target is compelled to laugh or scream uncontrollably (his choice) for 1 round, becoming incapacitated and unable to take reactions. Each target that fails also gains 1 stack of Setup.
Borrowed Smile. The Mocking King targets one creature within 30 feet that can see him. The target must succeed on a DC 18 Wisdom saving throw or be unable to distinguish the Mocking King from up to two illusory duplicates of himself, created by this action, until the end of its next turn. The duplicates occupy spaces within 10 feet of the Mocking King and vanish if hit.
The Shattered Masquerade Bleeds Through (Recharge 6). The Mocking King reaches for the boundary between Mirrorbloom and his realm and tears it thin. The same velvet ripple as his bright twin's ballroom spreads through a 30-foot radius centered on him, but the candlelight gutters sickly and the walls crack like old mirror-glass, frost-pattern fractures spreading outward from a single point. This transformation lasts until the end of the encounter. A Stitched Jester claws up through the broken glass and acts on the Mocking King's initiative count. Each enemy in the area must succeed on a DC 18 Wisdom saving throw or gain 1 free stack of Setup (see Setting Up the Bit) with no attack required; the cracked mirrors throughout the area also grant the Mocking King advantage on Stealth checks made to reposition unseen.
Legendary Actions

The Mocking King can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. He regains spent legendary actions at the start of his turn.

Silent Step. The Mocking King moves up to half his speed without provoking opportunity attacks, and this movement is silent.
Claw. The Mocking King makes one Cruel Claws attack.
Read the Room (Costs 2 Actions). The Mocking King studies a creature he can see within 60 feet, granting it 1 stack of Setup with no attack required.
