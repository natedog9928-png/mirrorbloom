Fair Quarry. The Hunter King can take the Dash, Disengage, or Dodge action as a bonus action on each of his turns. He never has advantage on attack rolls against a creature that is fleeing from him, fallen, or otherwise unable to meaningfully contest him — he considers such a kill no victory at all.
Sound the Horn. As an action, the Hunter King can sound his hunting horn. Any creature he has reduced to half its hit points or fewer that is within 60 feet and can hear the horn may immediately choose to yield. A creature that yields falls prone, drops anything it's holding, and is no longer a target of the Hunter King's attacks for the remainder of the encounter, provided it takes no further hostile action against him or his allies; the Hunter King will not pursue a creature that has yielded, and treats any further attack against it (by anyone) as a serious breach of the hunt's honor. This is not an illusion or a trick — the mercy is genuine and binding on his part.
Tireless Pursuer. The Hunter King is immune to the effects of exhaustion and ignores difficult terrain while moving toward a creature he can see, having never once lost a fair chase to mere ground.
Legendary Resistance (3/Day). If the Hunter King fails a saving throw, he can choose to succeed instead.
Actions
Multiattack. The Hunter King makes two Hunting Spear attacks.
Hunting Spear. Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack: +12 to hit, reach 10 ft. or range 30/120 ft., one target. Hit: 21 (3d8 + 8) piercing damage.
The Chase Begins (Recharge 5–6). The Hunter King chooses one creature he can see within 60 feet and begins a fair chase. Until the end of the encounter, the Hunter King has advantage on Wisdom (Survival) checks to track that creature, and the first attack he makes against it each round has advantage. This effect can be active on only one creature at a time, and ends immediately if that creature yields via Sound the Horn or is reduced to 0 hit points.
The Evergreen Run Bleeds Through (Recharge 6). The Hunter King reaches for the boundary between Mirrorbloom and his realm and tears it thin. Within a 30-foot radius centered on him, autumn leaves skitter across the ground from nowhere, the surrounding walls or terrain receding into distant tree-line as golden late-day light floods in sideways, the area's edges simply ceasing to be barriers. This transformation lasts until the end of the encounter. A Hound of the Open Field bounds out of the new tree-line and acts on the Hunter King's initiative count. Any creature in the area, ally or enemy, that chooses to flee the encounter outright may do so completely unimpeded — the Hunter King will not pursue, and instructs his allies not to either, a genuine retreat being, to him, its own kind of victory.
Legendary Actions

The Hunter King can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. He regains spent legendary actions at the start of his turn.

Run. The Hunter King moves up to his speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
Throw. The Hunter King makes one Hunting Spear attack.
Mark the Trail (Costs 2 Actions). The Hunter King designates a creature within 60 feet as his quarry for The Chase Begins, even if that trait has already recharged this encounter, by spending his own effort rather than waiting for it.
