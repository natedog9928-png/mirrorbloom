The Primordial Era

Before the Sundering — Year 0 — Year ~2,000 P.S. (Pre-Sundering)

Before the Sundering, the world was round.

Key Events

~Year 0
The First Dawn
The earliest moment any historical record attempts to describe. The theologian Arven Solis wrote in her Commentaries on Absence that 'the First Dawn was not the beginning of light but the beginning of the awareness that light existed, which is a different and more significant thing.' Her work is required reading at the Hopelight Institution of Mages and considered heresy by three of the four major religious orders.
~Year 200–800
The Age of Unity
The long middle period of the Primordial Era in which the single empire is believed to have been at its height. Architecture from this period — identified by the specific curvature in its stone foundations, designed for a round world — survives in fragments across Solance. The largest intact structure is the Vault of Aetherin in the Greenbelt Spires of the Shining Everlast, which predates the empire around it by approximately 7,000 years and which no one has yet successfully excavated past the third sub-level.
~Year 1,400
The First Recorded Myth: The Two Hands
The oldest surviving myth, preserved in oral tradition across at least eleven distinct cultures, tells of 'the Two Hands of the World' — two aspects of a single being that maintained the world's balance by never letting go of each other. The myth does not agree on what happens when they let go. Some versions say one hand slipped. Some say one was pulled away. Some say both chose it, for reasons the myth declines to explain. Scholars note that the disagreement between versions is itself informative.
~Year 1,800–2,000
Signs Before the Sundering
Several historical fragments describe a period of increasing instability before the Sundering — storms with no meteorological cause, animals behaving as though fleeing something without direction, a specific quality of silence in the hours before dawn that people at the time apparently found noteworthy enough to record. The historian Brek Ashvalen called this period 'the world holding its breath,' a phrase that has proven difficult to dislodge from the literature despite being, as multiple subsequent scholars have noted, not a rigorous description.
~Year 2,000
The Sundering
The world split. The round became flat. The single became two: Solance above, Umbrasol below. The goddess divided into the Great Hope and the Great Despair. The Fey Lords were born from the moment of breaking — six pairs, each a fragment of what the goddess was, splintered into top and shadow halves. The Unnamed Empire ceased to exist in the same moment, its peoples scattered across a world that no longer had the shape they had been built for. The Sundering is Year 0 of the 1st Age. Everything before it is measured in P.S. — Pre-Sundering. Everything after is measured by Age and year within it.

Named Figures

Arven Solis — Theologian of the early 4th Age. Author of Commentaries on Absence, the most cited and most argued-with text on Primordial Era theology. Died still writing the fourth volume.
Brek Ashvalen — Historian of the 3rd Age who coined 'the world holding its breath' and spent the last decade of his career trying to live it down. His actual scholarship on pre-Sundering archaeology is considered foundational.

Myths & Legends

The Two Hands — The oldest surviving myth. Two aspects of one being that maintained the world by never letting go. Eleven variants. No consensus on what caused them to separate.
The Vault of Aetherin — A structure in the Shining Everlast predating the current empire by 7,000 years. No one has excavated past the third sub-level. The institution that tried most recently stopped publishing updates about why they stopped.

The First Age

The Age of Ash — Year 1 — Year ~2,000 (1st Age)

The world that existed after the Sundering was not the world that existed before it.

Key Events

Year 1–100 (1st Age)
The Scattering
The immediate aftermath of the Sundering. Survivors of the Unnamed Empire found themselves in a flat world with none of the infrastructure — physical, political, or spiritual — that had sustained them. Population estimates for the end of the Scattering range from 40,000 to 200,000 total across all surviving peoples. The lower estimate is considered more likely by current scholarship. The flat world's geography was entirely new — mountains, rivers, and coastlines that had no relationship to anything the survivors had known.
Year 80
The First Doorway is Opened
The first recorded contact with the Fey Lords. A survivor community in what is now the Greenbelt Spires encountered a doorway to the First Morning — the Dawnward King's realm — and several members entered and returned. Their account, preserved in the Document of Seven Steps (named for the seven survivors who signed it), is the oldest first-hand description of a Fey Lord. The Dawnward King is described as 'enormous and golden and entirely unable to tell us how long we had been gone, which he seemed to find as interesting as we found it troubling.'
Year 100–300
The Unnamed Extinctions
The period in which at least thirty distinct peoples of the old world died out entirely. Some died in the Scattering. Some survived the Scattering and were killed in the Warlord conflicts that followed. Some simply declined — their populations too small, their knowledge too specialized for the world they now inhabited, their will exhausted. The Mirhen are the most documented. The Solvari, the Ashenkind, and the Pale Weavers are known by name only. The rest are archaeological.
Year 150
The First Warlords
As survivor groups found each other, conflict followed resources. The first named warlord in the historical record is Gorrath the Unsatisfied, whose title was apparently self-given and whose campaign to control the eastern water sources of what is now the Lamplight Quarter region lasted forty years and ended with his death at the hands of his own lieutenants. He is considered the template for the Warlord Century's general character: brutal, locally effective, strategically shortsighted, and ultimately temporary.
Year 300–800
The Warlord Century (Extended)
The misnomer is historical — it lasted closer to five hundred years. The period is characterized by constant low-level conflict between warlord-led groups, punctuated by brief periods of larger-scale war when two warlords with genuine strategic vision encountered each other. Population slowly recovered during this period despite the violence, which historians attribute to the fact that the warlords, whatever else they did, were effective at protecting food supplies and defending against the environmental dangers of the new flat world.
Year 900
The Covenant of the Eleven
The first formal peace agreement of the 1st Age. Eleven warlord-leaders of the central plains region agreed to stop fighting each other and begin trading instead. The Covenant lasted sixty years before collapsing, which is considered a remarkable achievement given the circumstances. Three of its signatories went on to found the earliest proto-kingdoms that would eventually become the kingdoms of the 2nd Age. The document itself survives in the Gilded Goose's restricted archive — one of the oldest original documents in the Shining Everlast.
Year 1,200
The First Maps of Solance
The cartographer Thessaly Vorn produced the first surviving map of Solance as a flat world — the first document to treat the flatness as a known fact rather than a suspected condition. Her maps were widely copied and are still used as the basis for modern cartography. She also produced a companion document, The Shape of the Below, speculating on the existence and nature of Umbrasol, which was considered eccentric at the time and is now considered prescient.
Year 1,800
The Long Peace Begins
The final two centuries of the 1st Age are called the Long Peace — a relative term, meaning 'significantly less violent than the eight centuries preceding it.' Population had recovered to perhaps 15% of pre-Sundering levels. Proto-kingdoms controlled most of the flat world's habitable regions. The conditions for the 2nd Age were in place. The transition is marked by historians at the point when the last purely warlord-led polity was absorbed into a proto-kingdom, an event that occurred without ceremony and was not recognized as significant until approximately three hundred years later.

Named Figures

Gorrath the Unsatisfied — The first named warlord in the historical record. Self-titled. Died at the hands of his own lieutenants after a forty-year campaign. Used as a cautionary example in 4th Age military academies.
Thessaly Vorn — Cartographer of the late 1st Age. Produced the first surviving accurate map of flat Solance and the first serious speculative document about Umbrasol's existence. Her The Shape of the Below is now required reading at the Hopelight Institution.
The Seven Signatories — The seven survivors who signed the Document of Seven Steps — the oldest first-hand account of a Fey Lord encounter. Their names are preserved: Arrath, Solvaine, Gresk, Mira of the Shore, Tollen, the one recorded only as 'the one who would not give their name,' and Fess.

Myths & Legends

The Gorrath Cycle — A body of oral tradition around Gorrath the Unsatisfied that gradually transformed him from a brutal warlord into a tragic figure — a man trying to hold together what the Sundering broke. Historians consider this transformation illustrative of how the 1st Age processed its own violence. The Cycle is still performed in some rural communities of the Shining Everlast.
The Document of Seven Steps — Treated by some religious traditions as a sacred text — the first mortal contact with a Fey Lord recorded in detail. The passage most frequently cited: 'He asked us what we remembered of before. We told him. He said he was sorry we remembered, because for him every morning was the first morning and he had not known there was a before to be sorry about.'

The Second Age

The Age of Kingdoms — Year 1 — Year ~2,000 (2nd Age)

The 2nd Age is the age that Solance chose to become after the survival of the 1st Age confirmed that survival was possible.

Key Events

Year 50–200 (2nd Age)
The Great Founding
The period in which the majority of the 2nd Age's kingdoms were established. Many had their roots in the proto-kingdoms of the late 1st Age. Others were founded by single individuals whose names are now attached to the kingdoms that outlasted them. The most famous founding of this period is the Kingdom of Vel, established by the twin sisters Velara and Velsin, whose disagreement about governance resulted in two separate kingdoms sharing one name for sixty years before being reunified by their grandchildren.
Year 300
The First Mages' Compact
Seven of the largest kingdoms agreed to share arcane research and forbid the weaponization of certain categories of magic. The Compact lasted four hundred years before fragmenting, which is considered the longest sustained multilateral agreement in 2nd Age history. Its influence on 4th Age magical law is direct — the Hopelight Institution's founding charter explicitly references the Compact's principles.
Year 450
The Cartography Wars
A forty-year conflict between six kingdoms over the right to produce official maps of Solance. On its surface absurd. In practice, the kingdom that controlled the maps controlled trade routes, border disputes, and access to Fey Lord doorways. The Cartography Wars established the principle — still operative in the 4th Age — that geographic knowledge is political power. The wars ended with a shared mapping commission that produced the Standard Atlas of Solance, a document still cited in legal disputes.
Year 700
The Founding of the First University
The University of the Still Lake, established in the central plains, is the oldest continuously operating academic institution in Solance. It was founded by the scholar-king Areth Voss, who famously refused the title of king after founding it and insisted on being addressed as 'the first student,' a tradition the university maintains symbolically by leaving his chair empty at the head of the faculty table. The University of the Still Lake was destroyed and rebuilt three times during the 3rd Age invasion and is currently the oldest building in what is now a minor province of the Shining Everlast.
Year 900
The Great Theological Debate
A formal academic debate, hosted by twelve kingdoms over the course of twenty years, on the nature of the Fey Lords and their relationship to the goddesses. The debate produced no consensus but did produce the Twelve Positions, a document that organized every major theological stance on the question into a coherent framework. Every religious tradition in the 4th Age traces its lineage to one of the Twelve Positions, even the ones that claim not to.
Year 1,200
The First Umbrasol Incursions
The first recorded crossings from Umbrasol into Solance. Small groups — scouting parties, the 3rd Age historians would later call them, though this implies a coordination that wasn't yet present. They appeared at the borders of seven kingdoms simultaneously, which suggested either coincidence or a level of planning Solance wasn't prepared to attribute to whatever was below. They killed, withdrew, and came back. The kingdoms they touched responded individually, which is to say inadequately.
Year 1,400–1,600
The Unification Attempts
Three separate attempts by dominant kingdoms to unite Solance against the growing incursion threat. The first, led by the Kingdom of Ashenvale, failed due to border disputes before it reached the negotiating stage. The second, led by the twin kingdoms of Vel, produced a treaty signed by forty-one kingdoms that lasted eleven years. The third, the Council of the Open Sky, produced a defensive alliance of eighty kingdoms that functioned until the 3rd Age invasion made it simultaneously necessary and insufficient.
Year 1,800
The Last Great Kingdom
The Kingdom of Solmere, the largest single polity of the 2nd Age at its peak, formally acknowledged the Umbrasol threat in a public declaration that is now called the Solmere Warning. Its queen, Taveth the Clear-Eyed, said in the declaration: 'We have been watching the below for two centuries and calling what we saw a problem for our children. Our children are us now.' The Solmere Warning is dated as the unofficial beginning of the 3rd Age, though the formal beginning is marked at the first coordinated invasion.

Named Figures

Velara and Velsin — The twin founders of the Kingdom of Vel. Their disagreement about governance philosophy resulted in sixty years of dual kingdoms sharing one name. Used in 4th Age political philosophy courses as a case study in founding conflicts.
Areth Voss — Scholar-king who founded the University of the Still Lake and refused the title of king afterward. His empty chair at the faculty table remains a tradition. Considered the father of formal academic culture in Solance.
Taveth the Clear-Eyed — Final queen of the Kingdom of Solmere. Her Solmere Warning is considered the clearest political document of the late 2nd Age and one of the most quoted texts in 4th Age military history. Died in the first year of the 3rd Age invasion defending the capital.

Myths & Legends

The Twelve Positions — The output of the Great Theological Debate — not a myth exactly, but treated as sacred text by most of Solance's religious traditions. Every major faith traces its lineage to one of the twelve stances on the nature of the goddesses and Fey Lords outlined in the document.
The Vel Paradox — A philosophical thought experiment derived from Velara and Velsin's split: if two rulers with opposite philosophies can produce the same good kingdom, what does that say about the philosophy? Used in ethics courses at the University of the Still Lake.

The Third Age

The Age of Shadows — Year 1 — Year ~2,000 (3rd Age)

Umbrasol did not announce itself. It arrived.

Key Events

Year 1 (3rd Age)
The First Night
The coordinated invasion of Solance by Umbrasol forces. Hundreds of simultaneous crossing points. No warning. The scholarly consensus is that the First Night was planned over centuries — the incursions of the late 2nd Age were intelligence gathering, not raiding. The Kingdom of Solmere fell in the first week. Queen Taveth the Clear-Eyed died defending its capital. Her last recorded words, preserved by a surviving guard, were: 'I said so.'
Year 1–100 (3rd Age)
The Century of Falling Kingdoms
Two hundred kingdoms fell in the first century. Some were destroyed. Some were absorbed. Some simply ceased to function as their populations fled northward toward higher ground and more defensible terrain. The University of the Still Lake was destroyed for the first time in Year 12 and rebuilt by Year 40. It was destroyed again in Year 67.
Year 30
First Understanding of Umbrasol
The scholar Pressa of Vel, working from captured documents and interrogation of Umbrasol prisoners, produced the first academic description of Umbrasol and the Law of Reflection. Her document, On the Nature of the Below, was the first text to use the word 'reflection' to describe the relationship between Solance and Umbrasol. It was also the first to suggest that fighting Umbrasol with pure force was strategically equivalent to hitting a mirror and being surprised by the cuts. Pressa was killed before she could publish a follow-up.
Year 150
The Fey Lords Reveal Their Knowledge
Under the pressure of the invasion, the Fey Lords — specifically the Hearthkeeper and the Hunter King, acting independently — provided Solance's surviving kingdoms with information about Umbrasol's structure and the nature of the Hollow Host and the Stalking King. This was the first time the Fey Lords had proactively shared information about their shadow counterparts. Theological scholars note that they shared tactical information but declined to explain the Law of Reflection in full or discuss the Sundering. The reason for this distinction has never been established.
Year 200–1,200
The Long Resistance
A thousand years of holding action. Solance did not advance. Umbrasol did not finally win. The surviving kingdoms, reduced to approximately sixty, held a defensive perimeter that shifted constantly but never collapsed entirely. The cost in lives across the Long Resistance is estimated at somewhere between thirty and fifty million — numbers that lose meaning at scale but that historians insist on citing anyway, because the loss of meaning is itself the point.
Year 400
The Council of Sixty
The sixty surviving kingdoms formally united into a single defensive council — the most successful political unification in Solance's history, achieved under the worst possible circumstances. The Council of Sixty is the direct ancestor of the 4th Age's fifteen empires. Its first Warden was the half-giant warrior-queen Solvaine Arrath, whose name combines those of two of the seven signatories of the Document of Seven Steps in a coincidence that theologians have been arguing about for fifteen hundred years.
Year 800
The First True Victory
The Battle of the Amber Field, in which a combined force of twelve kingdoms under General Toven Rek defeated a major Umbrasol advance and held the line for the first time in six centuries. Toven Rek was twenty-three years old. She lived to be ninety-one and is buried in the Amber Field, which is now a memorial site in the eastern provinces of the Shining Everlast. Her tactical manual, The Hard Way, is still required reading at the Shining Everlast's military colleges.
Year 1,200–1,800
The Counteroffensive
Six centuries of slow, costly advancement. The Council of Sixty, now reorganized into proto-empires, pushed Umbrasol's forces back step by step using tactics developed across the Long Resistance. The key breakthrough was the Reflection Strategy, developed by the arcane researcher Coris Vane: rather than fighting Umbrasol forces directly, Solance troops used mirror-light techniques that exploited the Law of Reflection against the invaders. The strategy took fifty years to implement at scale and turned the war.
Year 1,600
The Great Sealing
The largest magical working in Solance's history. A coalition of mages from forty proto-empires worked for eleven years to seal the majority of Umbrasol's crossing points — not permanently, but sufficiently to prevent coordinated mass invasion. The Great Sealing holds to the present day, though scholars debate whether it is weakening. The mages who performed it did not survive it. Their names are on the Wall of the Sealed in the Shining Everlast's Crownspires.
Year 1,800
The Great Repulsion
The final coordinated push that drove Umbrasol's remaining forces back below. Not a complete victory — Umbrasol still exists, the crossing points still function, the threat is permanent — but a decisive enough victory that the 3rd Age formally ended and the 4th Age began. The Repulsion was led by the Warden-General Greska Voss, descendant of the 2nd Age's Areth Voss. She named the fifteen territories that would become the 4th Age's empires in her victory address, a document called the Naming, which is read aloud in the Shining Everlast's Senate chamber every year on the anniversary of the Repulsion.

Named Figures

Pressa of Vel — Scholar who first described Umbrasol academically and coined the term 'reflection' for the relationship between the two halves. Killed before completing her work. On the Nature of the Below survives in fragments; scholars have spent three centuries trying to reconstruct the missing sections.
Solvaine Arrath — First Warden of the Council of Sixty. Half-giant. Named in a coincidence that combines the names of two of the original Document of Seven Steps signatories. Her descendants are a major noble family in the modern Shining Everlast.
Toven Rek — Twenty-three-year-old general who won the first major Solance victory at the Battle of the Amber Field. Her tactical manual The Hard Way remains required reading in military institutions. Buried at the Amber Field memorial.
Coris Vane — Arcane researcher who developed the Reflection Strategy — using the Law of Reflection against Umbrasol forces. The strategy took fifty years to implement and turned the war. Considered one of the most important figures in Solance's history by scholars who specialize in the 3rd Age.
Greska Voss — Warden-General who led the Great Repulsion. Named the fifteen future empires in her victory address. Her Naming is read aloud in the Senate every year. She lived twelve years after the Repulsion and spent them writing a memoir she declared unfinished and asked to be buried with it.

Myths & Legends

The First Night — The First Night has entered Solance's mythology as a symbol of sudden, total loss — the thing that comes without warning and changes everything. 'A First Night' is a common phrase in 4th Age Solance, meaning a catastrophic and irreversible change. Some traditions hold a yearly observance of mourning on its anniversary.
Toven Rek at Twenty-Three — The story of Toven Rek's age at the Amber Field has become a cultural touchstone — the idea that the worst moments produce the people capable of meeting them. Used in coming-of-age traditions in several provinces and quoted in approximately one third of 4th Age graduation addresses.
The Unnamed Mages of the Sealing — The mages who performed the Great Sealing and did not survive it are a major subject of religious veneration. Several traditions hold that their sacrifice was the act that most closely mirrored the goddesses' original unity — giving everything to hold the world together. The Wall of the Sealed is one of the most visited religious sites in the Shining Everlast.

The Fourth Age

The Age of Empires — Year 1 — Present (~Year 400, 4th Age)

The 4th Age began with the Repulsion and has not yet ended.

Key Events

Year 1 (4th Age)
The Naming and the Repulsion
Warden-General Greska Voss's victory address names fifteen territories as the future empires of Solance. The document is called the Naming. It is read aloud in the Shining Everlast's Senate every year. The fifteen names given are not all the names currently used — three empires have renamed themselves in the four centuries since, and two have merged and split again — but the number fifteen has held.
Year 1–50 (4th Age)
The Rebuilding
The first fifty years of the 4th Age were consumed by reconstruction. The 3rd Age had reduced Solance's built environment to perhaps a third of its 2nd Age peak. Libraries were the first priority — the loss of knowledge during the Long Resistance was considered the most dangerous long-term consequence of the invasion. The Hopelight Institution of Mages was founded in Year 23 of the 4th Age, explicitly as a repository for arcane knowledge that had nearly been lost.
Year 50
The Senate of the Shining Everlast Founded
The governing body of the largest empire was established by its first Speaker, Arren of the Long Table — a name chosen deliberately to invoke the Hearthkeeper, whose court had been a consistent ally during the Long Resistance. The Senate's founding document, the Charter of Open Deliberation, establishes the principle that all six factions must be represented regardless of military dominance, a principle that has survived four centuries of pressure to change it.
Year 100
The First Reflection Studies Department
The University of the Still Lake (rebuilt for the third and final time in Year 15 of the 4th Age) established the first formal academic department dedicated to the study of Umbrasol, the Law of Reflection, and the relationship between the two halves of the world. It was funded by the Shining Everlast's Senate over the objection of three factions who considered the study too dangerous. It has been the most productive academic department in Solance's history.
Year 200
The Formal Mapping of the Fey Lord Doorways
A joint commission of twelve empires produced the Doorway Atlas — the first comprehensive map of known Fey Lord doorway locations in Solance. The Atlas has been updated eleven times since its first edition and is now maintained by the Gilded Goose's Scroll Division in partnership with the Hopelight Institution. It is the most practically useful document in Solance for anyone traveling through areas near Fey Lord territory.
Year 300
The Long Peace Treaty
The first formal agreement between all fifteen empires since the Council of Sixty. The Long Peace Treaty established the current system of inter-empire arbitration, the shared standards for dealing with Umbrasol incursions, and the principle that no empire may unilaterally seal or open a Fey Lord doorway. It has held for a hundred years with three amendments. The Senate of the Shining Everlast was the last empire to ratify it, by a margin of one vote.
Year 350
The Mirrorbloom Discovery
The discovery — or rather the formal academic acknowledgment — that the Fey Lords' realms collectively constitute a space called Mirrorbloom, distinct from both Solance and Umbrasol, accessible through their doorways and governed by its own rules. The scholar who named it, Denna Farreach, noted that it had effectively always existed and that calling its discovery a discovery was 'the academic equivalent of noticing a room has always had a ceiling.' The name stuck.
Year ~380
Crockett Arrives in the Shining Everlast
A goblin of approximately three hundred years of age arrives in the Shining Everlast through a Mirrorbloom doorway, carrying a history no one there knows and a conviction that existing institutions are failing the people inside them. Within six months he founds the Gilded Goose. The Senate's administrative committee sends its first letter of concern approximately three weeks after the founding. It will not be the last.
Year ~400 (Present)
The Current Day
The Shining Everlast is the most powerful empire in Solance, its Senate navigating the competing interests of six factions, its Hopelight Institution of Mages at the forefront of arcane research, and its streets containing, among other things, the Gilded Goose — a guild that has risen to the top five in record time, causes the government significant stress on a regular basis, is beloved by the public, and is run by a three-hundred-year-old goblin who has seen enough of history to know exactly which rules need breaking and why.

Named Figures

Arren of the Long Table — First Speaker of the Shining Everlast's Senate. Deliberately named himself after the Hearthkeeper's court in honor of the Long Resistance alliance. His Charter of Open Deliberation is the founding document of the Senate's structure.
Denna Farreach — Scholar who formally named Mirrorbloom as a distinct space and wrote the paper acknowledging that everyone had effectively always known it existed. Known for extremely dry academic prose.
The Founders of the Hopelight Institution — The twelve mages who established the Hopelight Institution of Mages in Year 23 of the 4th Age. Their portraits hang in the main hall. Anna Linfay's portrait, added after her departure, was hung facing the wall by person or persons unknown. It has been turned back around four times. It keeps ending up facing the wall.

Myths & Legends

The Naming — Greska Voss's victory address has acquired semi-sacred status — reading it aloud in the Senate is both a legal requirement and a religious observance in some traditions. Scholars note that she was exhausted when she gave it and several of the fifteen names were clearly improvised, which various traditions have found ways to interpret as significant.
Crockett's Arrival — Already the subject of embellishment in the four centuries since it occurred — most commonly the claim that Crockett arrived already knowing the name of everyone in the Senate, that he came through three different doorways simultaneously, or that the Dawnward King personally told him it was time to go. Crockett has confirmed none of these and denied two of them.
