Marked Quarry. As a bonus action, the Stalking King can mark one creature he can see within 60 feet as his quarry. Only one creature can be marked at a time; marking a new creature ends the previous mark. While a creature is marked: — The Stalking King always knows the marked creature's direction and distance, even through walls or magical concealment that doesn't explicitly block divination, and the marked creature can't benefit from being invisible or hidden from him. — The marked creature's speed can't be increased by any effect while within 120 feet of the Stalking King. — The Stalking King has advantage on attack rolls against the marked creature. — The mark can't be removed by the marked creature; it ends only when the Stalking King marks a different creature, is reduced to 0 hit points, or chooses to end it.
The Broken Horn. The Stalking King carries a horn identical to the Hunter King's, but it makes no sound. Any effect that would force, compel, or persuade the Stalking King to end a chase, grant mercy, or call off pursuit automatically fails; he is incapable of yielding the hunt by means even he no longer fully understands.
Tireless Pursuer. The Stalking King is immune to the effects of exhaustion and ignores difficult terrain while moving toward his marked quarry, having never once lost a chase to mere ground — fair or otherwise.
Legendary Resistance (3/Day). If the Stalking King fails a saving throw, he can choose to succeed instead.
Actions
Multiattack. The Stalking King makes two Notched Spear attacks.
Notched Spear. Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack: +12 to hit, reach 10 ft. or range 30/120 ft., one target. Hit: 24 (3d10 + 8) piercing damage, plus 9 (2d8) necrotic damage if the target is the Stalking King's marked quarry.
Unending Pursuit (Recharge 5–6). The Stalking King moves up to his speed toward his marked quarry without provoking opportunity attacks, ignoring difficult terrain, and makes one Notched Spear attack against it at the end of this movement.
The Frozen Run Bleeds Through (Recharge 6). The Stalking King reaches for the boundary between Mirrorbloom and his realm and tears it thin. The same tree-line that floods his bright twin's battlefields spreads through a 30-foot radius centered on him, but frost-killed and bare, the light gone flat and blue, the temperature dropping hard enough to fog breath mid-sentence. This transformation lasts until the end of the encounter. A Bone Hound claws up from the frozen ground and acts on the Stalking King's initiative count. While the area persists, his Marked Quarry trait's range becomes unlimited rather than 60 feet for the purpose of marking and tracking, and the transformed terrain is difficult only for creatures other than the Stalking King.
Legendary Actions

The Stalking King can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. He regains spent legendary actions at the start of his turn.

Close the Distance. The Stalking King moves up to his speed toward his marked quarry without provoking opportunity attacks.
Throw. The Stalking King makes one Notched Spear attack.
The Mark Tightens (Costs 2 Actions). The Stalking King's marked quarry must succeed on a DC 19 Wisdom saving throw or be frightened of him until the end of its next turn, despite his condition immunity to the same fear.
