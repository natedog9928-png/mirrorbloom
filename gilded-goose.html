The Gilded Goose

"Find it. Understand it. Share it. Break the rules that say you can't."

Top 5 active guilds in the Shining Everlast. Beloved by the public — the free meals, the open archive, the contracts they take that nobody else will. The Senate is split: half of them cannot stand the Gilded Goose, half find them genuinely amusing, and all of them know the guild is too valuable to do anything about. The experiments frequently get out of hand. There are disasters. The guild always cleans them up, which is reassuring, but not before the cleanup costs the government significant money, which is less reassuring. The Magical College has seventeen open complaints. Somehow the guild keeps getting invited to things.

Overview

The Gilded Goose is a research institution that learned early that research doesn't fund itself. Founded by Crockett — a goblin who has been alive for approximately three hundred years, by mechanisms he does not explain, whose time magic has grown from chaotic accident into something closer to a second language — the guild exists to find things, understand them, and share them. Not to monetize discoveries. Not to lock findings behind institutional access. To put what they learn back into the world.

The mercenary arm came second. Research requires travel, travel requires protection, and protection that understands what it's protecting is worth more than protection that doesn't. The Sword Division funds the Scroll Division. The Scroll Division gives the Sword Division better information than any other outfit in the Shining Everlast has access to.

Guildmaster

Founder — CR 25 (75,000 XP)

Crockett

Small humanoid (goblin), chaotic neutral

A small, very old goblin who wears his three hundred years the way a well-used tool wears its history — with evidence of everything it has been through and no apparent interest in pretending otherwise. Large white beard. Vestments modified over centuries to include pockets in dimensions that should not exist at this scale. His eyes have the quality of someone watching several timelines at once and choosing, moment to moment, which one to act in. He moves like someone who has made three hundred years of mistakes, learned from most of them, and is actively working on the rest.

Chaotic in the way of something that has always been chaotic and has simply had three centuries to develop opinions about it. He has not calmed down — he has decided which direction to be chaotic in. Irreverent, genuinely kind, prone to decisions that make Anna put her face in her hands. He takes in outcasts because he has never believed that the people institutions reject are the problem — he believes the institutions are. The guild reflects this completely. He still breaks rules. He is now three hundred years old and has broken enough of them to know which ones deserve it.

Swordmaster

Human — CR 10

A drunken fool by reputation and something close to that by choice — being underestimated is professionally useful. Beneath it is a heart of remarkable generosity and a tactical mind sharpened by decades of contracts that required more creativity than force. Medium humanoid (human), chaotic neutral — CR 13 (10,000 XP)

A lanky man with the distracted energy of someone whose mind is always three steps ahead of the room. White coat over whatever he last remembered to change into. Arcane lenses built into his eye sockets — they glow faintly blue and rotate when he is thinking hard, which is most of the time. Reinforc...

Brilliant, enthusiastic, and genuinely well-meaning in a way that does not fully account for how alarming most of his ideas are to people who haven't spent time around him. He never modifies someone without consent. He is very persuasive about obtaining that consent.

Headquarters — The Goose and Lantern

The Lamplight Quarter, The Shining Everlast

From the outside it is a building. From the inside it is significantly more building than that. The sign above the door — a gilded goose carrying a lantern, painted by Crockett himself on the first day — is charming rather than professional. Step inside and the geometry stops being reliable: the ground floor common room is larger than the building's exterior allows. The research floors above are larger still. The archive floors exceed what should be physically possible given the roof. Will has an entire laboratory wing on the third floor that does not correspond to any exterior window. Nobody has mapped the building completely. Orvo Nix tried once and produced a document he describes as a working hypothesis. Crockett has not explained this. He finds the question mildly interesting.

The Common Room

Open to the public. Free meals for anyone who brings something worth learning. Consistently the most interesting place to have dinner in the Lamplight Quarter.

Will's Workshop

Third floor, marked KNOCK AND WAIT. Always occupied. Always mid-project.

The Open Archive

Fifth floor. Freely accessible to credentialed visitors. Complete catalogue of guild publications.

The Restricted Archive

Sixth floor. Lorekeeper rank or active partnership required. Incomplete findings and research awaiting verification.

Crockett's Office

Eighth floor. Seventeen active projects, a board covered in string, and a door that opens differently each visit. Visitors welcome. The wait is sometimes longer than expected.

Culture

The Gilded Goose has a reputation that contains a contradiction, and the contradiction is the point.

From the outside: a guildmaster who has been alive three hundred years and still approaches problems like they are personally interesting. A head of research who left the most prestigious institution in the city and considers this an upgrade. A swordmaster visibly drunk at most morning briefings who remains the most tactically dangerous person in any room he occupies. A head of administration who is small, blonde, green-eyed, and gentle, and runs the guild's finances with the precision of someone who knows exactly how much everything costs, including the things that aren't supposed to have a price.
