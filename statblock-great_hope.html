Languages all, telepathy 120 ft.
Radiant Field. At the start of each creature's turn while that creature is within 30 feet of the Great Hope, that creature regains 10 hit points and has advantage on its next saving throw against being frightened or charmed before the start of its next turn. This applies to every creature regardless of allegiance, including those fighting the Great Hope.
Unbearable Sincerity. Whenever a creature within 60 feet of the Great Hope tells a deliberate lie, that creature must succeed on a DC 23 Charisma saving throw or be unable to speak anything but the truth for 1 minute. The Great Hope cannot suppress this trait; it is not a choice she makes.
Innate Spellcasting. The Great Hope's spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 23). She can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: At will: bless, guiding bolt, sanctuary 3/day each: greater restoration, heal, sunbeam 1/day each: mass heal, true resurrection
Magic Resistance. The Great Hope has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.
Legendary Resistance (4/Day). If the Great Hope fails a saving throw, she can choose to succeed instead.
Actions
Multiattack. The Great Hope makes two Radiant Touch attacks.
Radiant Touch. Melee Weapon Attack: +15 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 24 (3d10 + 8) radiant damage. A fiend or undead hit by this attack has disadvantage on its next saving throw before the end of its next turn.
Wave of Dawn (Recharge 5–6). The Great Hope releases a burst of golden light. Each creature within 30 feet of her must make a DC 23 Constitution saving throw, taking 36 (8d8) radiant damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one. Constructs, undead, and fiends have disadvantage on this save. Each ally of the Great Hope within range instead regains 36 hit points and is cured of one disease or condition from the following list: blinded, deafened, frightened, paralyzed, poisoned.
Compel Honesty. The Great Hope targets one creature she can see within 60 feet. The target must succeed on a DC 23 Wisdom saving throw or be compelled to answer the next question the Great Hope or her allies ask it truthfully, as per the zone of truth spell, with no additional save allowed.
Legendary Actions

The Great Hope can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The Great Hope regains spent legendary actions at the start of her turn.

Step of Light. The Great Hope flies up to half her flying speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
Touch. The Great Hope makes one Radiant Touch attack.
A Brighter Day (Costs 2 Actions). The Great Hope grants one creature she can see within 60 feet a burst of overwhelming hope. That creature regains 20 hit points, and if it is a creature fighting alongside the Great Hope's enemies, it must succeed on a DC 23 Wisdom saving throw or be unable to attack the Great Hope or her allies until the end of its next turn, frozen by sudden, unwanted clarity.
Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the Great Hope can take a lair action to cause one of the following effects; she can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

Meadow Breaks Through. Flowers and soft grass erupt from every surface within 60 feet of the Great Hope. The area becomes difficult terrain for creatures hostile to the Great Hope until initiative count 20 on the next round.
Golden Hour. Every creature within 60 feet of the Great Hope that she chooses regains 15 hit points.
Echo of What Was Lost. Until initiative count 20 on the next round, any creature that begins its turn within 60 feet of the Great Hope and has fewer than half its hit points remaining feels an inexplicable, aching warmth — that creature has advantage on its next death saving throw, if any.
