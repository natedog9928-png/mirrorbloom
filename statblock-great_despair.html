Despair Feeds. At the start of each of her turns, the Great Despair gains 10 temporary hit points for every creature within 60 feet of her that is frightened, has 0 hit points, or has failed a saving throw since her last turn. These temporary hit points do not stack with themselves and are recalculated each turn (use the higher value).
Crowned in Ruin. The Great Despair's AC increases by 1 (to a maximum of 25) for every creature that has dropped to 0 hit points in the current combat, as the crown she does not truly own grows more jagged and real in her grasping mind. This bonus resets at the end of combat.
The Lie of the Crown. Once per combat, if a creature succeeds on a DC 22 Charisma (Persuasion) check made as an action to speak aloud the truth that no crown was ever taken from her — that what she lost was love, not a throne — the Great Despair must succeed on a DC 22 Wisdom saving throw or become stunned until the end of her next turn, and all temporary hit points granted by Despair Feeds are immediately lost. She has advantage on this save if she has reduced a creature to 0 hit points in the last minute. This effect can only be attempted once per creature, ever — she does not forget who has tried to free her.
Innate Spellcasting. The Great Despair's spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 23). She can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: At will: detect thoughts, fear, mirror image 3/day each: dominate monster, eyebite, phantasmal killer 1/day each: weird, power word kill
Magic Resistance. The Great Despair has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.
Legendary Resistance (4/Day). If the Great Despair fails a saving throw, she can choose to succeed instead.
Actions
Multiattack. The Great Despair makes three attacks: one with Devouring Maw and two with Claws of the Forgotten Queen.
Devouring Maw. Melee Weapon Attack: +15 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 32 (5d10 + 5) piercing damage, and the target must succeed on a DC 23 Constitution saving throw or have its hit point maximum reduced by an amount equal to the damage taken. This reduction lasts until the target finishes a long rest. A creature reduced to 0 hit point maximum by this effect dies, its despair complete.
Claws of the Forgotten Queen. Melee Weapon Attack: +15 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 20 (3d8 + 7) slashing damage plus 14 (4d6) psychic damage.
Gaze of Six Sorrows (Recharge 5–6). The Great Despair fixes her six eyes on up to six creatures she can see within 60 feet of her. Each target must succeed on a DC 23 Wisdom saving throw or become frightened for 1 minute and take 27 (6d8) psychic damage (half as much on a successful save and the target is not frightened). A frightened creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns.
Whisper of the Crown. The Great Despair targets one creature she can see within 120 feet that can hear her. The target must succeed on a DC 23 Wisdom saving throw or become convinced, for 1 minute, that something irreplaceable has been stolen from it. While convinced, the creature has disadvantage on attack rolls against the Great Despair and advantage on attack rolls against its allies. A creature can repeat the saving throw whenever it takes damage from the Great Despair.
Legendary Actions

The Great Despair can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The Great Despair regains spent legendary actions at the start of her turn.

Step Through Shadow. The Great Despair moves up to her speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
Claw. The Great Despair makes one Claws of the Forgotten Queen attack.
Feed the Crown (Costs 2 Actions). The Great Despair targets one frightened creature she can see. The target must succeed on a DC 23 Wisdom saving throw or drop to half its current hit points, rounded down, as its hope is consumed.
Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the Great Despair can take a lair action to cause one of the following effects; she can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

The Walls Remember. Ghostly, screaming faces emerge from the nearest surface within 60 feet of the Great Despair. Each creature within 15 feet of that point must succeed on a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw or be frightened until the end of its next turn.
Hope Guttering. One creature within 60 feet of the Great Despair that the Great Despair can see must make a DC 20 Constitution saving throw, taking 18 (4d8) necrotic damage on a failure, or half as much on a success. If this damage reduces the target to half its hit points or fewer, the Great Despair gains 20 temporary hit points (separate from Despair Feeds).
Crooked Crown's Echo. Until initiative count 20 on the next round, the Great Despair's lair fills with whispered illusions of an unkept promise. Creatures other than the Great Despair have disadvantage on Wisdom saving throws while in the lair.
