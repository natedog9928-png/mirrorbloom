Nothing Forgotten. The first time in a combat that a creature deals damage to the Duskward King with a specific attack, spell, or ability, he gains resistance to that exact source for the remainder of the encounter (e.g., resistance to that specific spell, or to that creature's specific weapon attack). The first time a creature successfully imposes a condition on him, he gains advantage on saving throws against that condition for the remainder of the encounter. The Duskward King's player or the DM should track this openly — he remembers, and he wants you to know he remembers.
Echo of Endings. When a creature within 60 feet of the Duskward King drops to 0 hit points, he regains 20 hit points and his weapon attacks deal an additional 1d8 necrotic damage until the end of the encounter. This effect stacks.
Every Ending, Witnessed. The Duskward King has advantage on Wisdom (Insight) checks against any creature that has dropped to 0 hit points or fled combat at any point in the current encounter, having already seen exactly how that creature breaks.
Innate Spellcasting. The Duskward King's spellcasting ability is Intelligence (spell save DC 20). He can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: At will: chill touch, false life 3/day each: animate dead, bestow curse 1/day each: finger of death
Legendary Resistance (3/Day). If the Duskward King fails a saving throw, he can choose to succeed instead.
Actions
Multiattack. The Duskward King makes two attacks with Blade of the Final Hour.
Blade of the Final Hour. Melee Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 21 (3d8 + 8) necrotic damage. If this attack reduces a creature to half its hit points or fewer, that creature also has its speed halved until the end of its next turn, weighed down by sudden, total exhaustion.
Weight of Every Ending (Recharge 5–6). The Duskward King forces a target within 60 feet to relive every ending it has ever caused or witnessed. The target must make a DC 18 Wisdom saving throw, taking 27 (6d8) psychic damage on a failure, or half as much on a success. On a failure, the target also has disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks until the end of its next turn.
That Has Happened Before. The Duskward King targets one creature he can see within 60 feet that has used the same action (the same spell, the same weapon, the same special ability) more than once during this combat. That creature must succeed on a DC 18 Wisdom saving throw or be unable to use that specific action again for 1 minute, as the Duskward King's certainty that 'this has already failed once' becomes a kind of curse.
The Last Evening Bleeds Through (Recharge 6). The Duskward King reaches for the boundary between Mirrorbloom and his realm and tears it thin. Shadows lengthen unnaturally as the light itself seems to age in place; within a 30-foot radius centered on him, walls and floor take on the burnt, ash-streaked texture of a battlefield long since gone quiet. This transformation lasts until the end of the encounter. A Lingering Echo rises out of the floor like smoke given shape and acts on the Duskward King's initiative count. A creature in the area can spend an action to question the Echo and learn one true detail about a past event relevant to the current conflict, at the DM's discretion — but all creatures in the area, the Duskward King included, take a cumulative -1 penalty to attack rolls for each round they remain (maximum -3), as the weight of every ending presses down on everyone equally.
Legendary Actions

The Duskward King can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. He regains spent legendary actions at the start of his turn.

Slow March. The Duskward King moves up to half his speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
Final Strike. The Duskward King makes one Blade of the Final Hour attack.
Recall the Wound (Costs 2 Actions). The Duskward King targets a creature he has already damaged this combat. That creature takes 10 (3d6) necrotic damage as the Duskward King reminds it, precisely, of how it was hurt before.
