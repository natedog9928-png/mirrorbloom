Worn Faces. At the start of combat and as a bonus action on any of his turns, the Jester King changes which mask he wears, choosing one of the four below. He gains that mask's benefits until he changes again. Changing masks doesn't provoke opportunity attacks.
The Charming Face. AC 18. The Jester King has advantage on Charisma checks and Charisma saving throws, and creatures have disadvantage on saving throws against his spells while he wears this face.
The Quick Face. AC 16. The Jester King's speed increases to 50 feet, he gains an extra reaction each round, and he has advantage on Dexterity saving throws.
The Stone Face. AC 20. The Jester King has resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage from nonmagical attacks, but his speed is reduced to 20 feet.
The Watching Face. AC 17. The Jester King has truesight 60 feet and advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks, and he can't be surprised.
Never the Same Trick Twice. The Jester King has advantage on saving throws against any spell or effect that a creature has already used against him once this combat, as though the joke has already been told and he's heard the punchline coming.
Innate Spellcasting. The Jester King's spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 19). He can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: At will: disguise self, minor illusion, vicious mockery 3/day each: confusion, mislead 1/day each: mass suggestion
Legendary Resistance (3/Day). If the Jester King fails a saving throw, he can choose to succeed instead.
Actions
Multiattack. The Jester King makes two Cutting Wit attacks.
Cutting Wit. Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 30 ft., one target. Hit: 17 (3d6 + 7) psychic damage, as a barb of laughter and insult lands exactly where it hurts most.
Riddle With No Answer (Recharge 5–6). The Jester King poses an impossible riddle to up to three creatures he can see within 60 feet. Each target must succeed on a DC 19 Intelligence saving throw or be incapacitated until the end of its next turn, hopelessly turning the riddle over in its mind.
Swap the Mask. The Jester King magically exchanges appearances with a willing or charmed creature within 30 feet for 1 minute or until he chooses to end it. While swapped, attacks against either creature have disadvantage unless the attacker has true sight or other means to see through illusion.
The Masquerade Bleeds Through (Recharge 6). The Jester King reaches for the boundary between Mirrorbloom and his realm and tears it thin. Within a 30-foot radius centered on him, walls ripple like draped velvet, candlelight and distant music seeping in from nowhere as the edges of the room go soft and indistinct, a ballroom that was never built starting to overwrite the real one. This transformation lasts until the end of the encounter. A Masked Courtier steps out of a curtain that wasn't there a moment ago and acts on the Jester King's initiative count. Allies in the area can swap positions with any illusory dancer as a bonus action, blinking instantly to a new space within the area. Enemies in the area, however, must succeed on a DC 19 Wisdom saving throw at the start of their turn or target the nearest creature (ally or enemy) when they next make an attack, genuinely lost in the crowd.
Legendary Actions

The Jester King can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. He regains spent legendary actions at the start of his turn.

Vanishing Step. The Jester King teleports up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space he can see.
Jab. The Jester King makes one Cutting Wit attack.
Change of Face (Costs 2 Actions). The Jester King swaps masks (see Worn Faces) outside his normal turn.
