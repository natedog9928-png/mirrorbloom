The Great Despair

The Uncrowned Queen

God — Gargantuan fey, lawful evil — Challenge 24

She remembers the Breaking, or believes she does, which amounts to the same agony either way. In her telling — and it is the only telling the Hollow Court is permitted to repeat — a crown was taken from her in the moment of the world's splitting, a right, a throne, something owed that was stripped away by forces she can no longer name. She has spent every year since trying to reclaim it. There is no crown. There was never a crown. What she actually lost was the Great Hope herself, and somewhere beneath the six glowing eyes and the razor smile, a part of her may even understand this — which is precisely why the lie has to be so carefully, so violently maintained. She wears a twisted mockery of regalia, dresses for a war she's still fighting, and rules the Hollow Court not as a monarch enthroned but as a general still marching toward a victory that was never the actual goal.
